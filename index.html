Investor Philosophies
🎩 Warren Buffett
Seeks companies with durable competitive advantages (moats), consistent high ROE (>15%), low debt, and strong free cash flow. Buys at fair prices and holds forever.

Key metrics: ROE >15% · FCF Margin >10% · P/E <30 · D/E <2
🧠 Charlie Munger
Focuses on quality businesses with strong moats, pricing power, and high returns on capital. Willing to pay up for truly exceptional businesses.

Key metrics: ROE >20% · FCF Margin >15% · Clear moat
📈 Peter Lynch
Famous for "invest in what you know". Looks for undervalued growth stocks with P/E below 20. Loved small and mid-cap companies others overlooked.

Key metrics: P/E <20 · ROE >10% · PEG ratio <1
📐 Benjamin Graham
The "father of value investing". Sought stocks trading at a margin of safety below intrinsic value. Strict criteria: low P/E, dividend yield, strong balance sheet.

Key metrics: P/E <15 · Dividend >2% · D/E <1
💰 Dividend Income
Focus on companies with strong, growing dividends. Ideal for income investors seeking passive cash flow. Prioritises yield, payout sustainability, and stability.

Key metrics: Yield >2.5% · ROE >10% · Stable cash flows
🚀 High Growth
Targets companies with exceptional return on equity and strong free cash flow generation. Often technology and platform businesses compounding rapidly.

Key metrics: ROE >25% · FCF Margin >20%
KPI Glossary
ROE — Return on Equity
How much profit a company generates per dollar of shareholder equity. Buffett wants 15%+ consistently. High ROE = efficient use of capital.
FCF Margin — Free Cash Flow Margin
Percentage of revenue left as free cash after capital expenditure. Buffett loves cash-generating businesses. 15%+ is excellent.
P/E — Price to Earnings
How many years of earnings you pay for the stock. Graham wanted under 15. Buffett accepts up to 25-30 for quality businesses.
Debt/Equity Ratio
Company debt relative to equity. Buffett avoids over-leveraged businesses. Under 1.0 is conservative, under 2.0 is acceptable.
Dividend Yield
Annual dividend as % of stock price. Graham required dividends. Income investors target 3%+. High yield can signal distress — check sustainability.
Economic Moat
Buffett's term for durable competitive advantage — what protects a business from competition. Brand, network effects, switching costs, cost advantages.
Buffett Score (0–100)
Our composite quality score combining ROE, FCF margin, P/E, debt, and dividend yield — weighted as Buffett would. 80+ is exceptional quality.
